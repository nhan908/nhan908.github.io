Day-by-Day Itinerary

Tonight

Mumbai → Lonavala

Arrival night — check in, rest up

~95 km NH48 Expressway
Depart

Mumbai (Western Suburbs)

Take the Mumbai–Pune Expressway (NH48) — smooth 6-lane highway, well-lit at night. Expect a fuel stop at Khalapur or the Expressway service plaza. Toll booths are FASTag-enabled.

Arrive

Lonavala

~1.5 hrs from Mumbai at a steady pace. Check in, grab a hot cup of chai on the main market road. The town quiets down by 10pm so settle in early.

Night tip: Avoid the old Mumbai–Pune highway (via Khandala) at night — twisty, poor lighting. Stick to the Expressway tonight.
Day 1

Lonavala Base Day — Viewpoints & Caves

A relaxed loop of the plateau's greatest hits

~75 km Loop · Stay: Lonavala
Morning Start

Lonavala → Duke's Nose

Ride east on the old Mumbai–Pune highway (NH48 old alignment). Duke's Nose is a short 1km trail from the road — a dramatic rocky outcrop with sheer drops and sweeping Khopoli valley views. Best at sunrise. 15 min ride.

Viewpoint

Tiger's Leap (Waghdari)

A 650m sheer cliff shaped like a leaping tiger. One of the most dramatic viewpoints in the Sahyadris. The Himalayan parks easily here. 5 min from Duke's Nose.

Viewpoint

Lion's Point & Sunset Point

Sweeping 180° views of three valleys converging. The road up is lined with corn and bhutta vendors. Arrive before 9am to beat tour groups.

Brunch Stop

Bhushi Dam Area

Return to Lonavala for breakfast at one of the dhabas near Bhushi Dam. Try misal pav and chai. The dam itself is a classic stop — though it's best during monsoon, the area is scenic year-round.

Heritage

Karla Caves

Ride east ~15km on the old highway. The Chaitya Griha at Karla is the largest rock-cut Buddhist prayer hall in India — over 2,000 years old. The Himalayan fits in the small parking area. ₹40 entry.

Heritage

Bhaja Caves

3km from Karla, slightly off the road. 22 rock-cut caves from the 2nd century BC with a beautiful carved chaitya and a waterfall nearby. Far less crowded than Karla.

Optional

Lohagad Fort (if energy permits)

A 20-minute ride from Bhaja, then a 45-min hike up to a perfectly preserved hill fort with stunning valley views. The dirt road to the base trailhead is ideal Himalayan territory. Skip if tired — save legs for Day 2.

Evening

Back to Lonavala — Market Walk

Hit the main bazaar for chikki and fudge (Lonavala's two great exports). Cooper's and Maganlal are the gold-standard shops. Get back before dark and pick up supplies for the next day's longer ride.

Day 2

The Ghat Descent — Lonavala to Kolad

Down the Borghat, through the Konkan to the river

~120 km Ghats + Konkan · Stay: Kolad
Morning

Lonavala → Khandala → Borghat Descent

Take the old Mumbai–Pune highway down the Borghat (not the Expressway). This is the classic British-era switchback road — tight hairpins, dramatic valley views on every bend. The Himalayan was made for this. Take it slowly; trucks share the road.

Descent

Khopoli — Base of the Ghats

At the foot of the ghats, the landscape shifts suddenly to flat Konkan. Fuel up here — next good fuel point is Kolad. Take the NH66 south (the old coastal highway).

Konkan Run

NH66 South → Pen → Nagothane

Smooth, fast road through mango orchards, rice paddies, and Konkan villages. The air quality shifts noticeably — cooler, humid, more fragrant. Look out for the Patalganga river crossing near Khopoli.

Arrive ~Noon

Kolad — Riverside Camp

Kolad sits on the Kundalika River and is Maharashtra's best-known white-water rafting base. Check in early, freshen up, then book an afternoon rafting session (Grade 3–4, ~₹800–1,200 per head) — the adrenaline pairs well with a day of riding. Evening is quiet around the river with fireflies after dark.

Alternative scenic route (add 40km): From Lonavala, head south via the Expressway to Pune, then west through Paud and down the Tamhini Ghat to Kolad. Tamhini is arguably Maharashtra's most beautiful ghat road — narrow, canopied, with waterfalls running across the tarmac in season. Adds 1.5 hrs but is unforgettable.
Day 3

River to Expressway — Kolad → Mumbai

Easy return via coastal highway and the expressway

~195 km NH66 + NH48
Morning

Kolad → Nagothane → Pen

Depart by 8am. Retrace north on NH66 — the morning light on the Konkan plains is lovely. Stop for vada pav and chai at a roadside stall in Pen or Nagothane.

Optional Detour

Khopoli → Rajmachi Village (Dirt Road)

If you're up for it: from Khopoli, a 22km off-road trail leads up to Rajmachi village at the base of Rajmachi Fort — proper Himalayan terrain (rocky, forested, stream crossings). Return to Khopoli after, then join the Expressway. Allow 2 hrs extra.

Highway

Khopoli → Mumbai-Pune Expressway → Mumbai

Join the Expressway at Khalapur and cruise back to Mumbai. The Himalayan is comfortable at 90–100 km/h. Aim to reach Mumbai before midday to avoid evening traffic.

Final Meal

Khopoli / Khalapur — Dhaba Lunch

Several excellent highway dhabas at the Khopoli exit serve thali and fresh Konkan seafood curries. A fitting end before the city swallows you.

Where to Stay

LONAVALA — Nights 0 & 1

Budget · ₹1,500–2,800

Hotel Adarsh / Hotel Chandralok

Clean, central, no-frills rooms on the main bazaar road. Motorbike parking on the street outside. Good for an early-morning departure.

Mid-range · ₹4,500–8,000

Fariyas Resort

Well-maintained resort with a pool, restaurant and covered bike parking. Walking distance from the market. Reliable WiFi for route planning.

Splurge · ₹12,000–20,000

The Machan

Stilted treehouse eco-resort in the forest canopy above Lonavala. Utterly unique — no TVs, no roads audible. Book the Canopy Machan well in advance.

KOLAD — Night 2

Camp · ₹1,800–3,000

Kundalika River Camp / Nirvana Adventures

Riverside tent camps with bonfires, rafting packages included in some deals. Basic but atmospheric — stars are spectacular this far from the city.

Mid · ₹3,500–6,000

River Retreat Heritage Resort

The most comfortable option in Kolad — proper beds, a veranda over the river, and decent food. Secure bike parking. Book 2 days ahead on weekends.

Alternative

Atali Ganga (Aamby)

Slightly further north near Khopoli, but upscale glamping tents on the riverside. Good if the Kolad camps are full on a holiday weekend.

What to Eat

🍬

Chikki & Fudge

Lonavala's most famous exports — brittle peanut or cashew candy (chikki) and dense milk fudge. Non-negotiable purchase.

Cooper's Chikki · Maganlal Chikki · Lonavala Fudge Centre — all on the main bazaar
🌽

Bhutta (Roasted Corn)

Charcoal-roasted corn rubbed with lime and chilli — a Sahyadri staple sold at every viewpoint, especially Lion's Point.

Lion's Point · Tiger's Leap · Bhushi Dam vendors
🍛

Misal Pav

Spicy sprouted bean curry with a pav roll — the quintessential Maharashtra breakfast. Lonavala's dhabas do excellent versions.

Hotel Shreeji (Lonavala) · Any dhaba near Bhushi Dam
🫙

Vada Pav & Chai

Maharashtra's greatest roadside meal. The journey from Khopoli to Kolad is lined with outstanding vada pav stalls.

NH66 roadside stalls · Pen · Nagothane market
🦀

Konkan Seafood Thali

Once you're in the Konkan (post-ghat descent), fresh pomfret, surmai, and crab curries with kokum sol kadhi are abundant and excellent.

Dhabas in Pen or Nagothane · Kolad market eateries
☕

Cutting Chai at Sunrise

Every ghat viewpoint has a chai vendor by 6:30am. The combination of a hot glass of cutting chai with a valley full of morning mist is genuinely unbeatable.

Duke's Nose trailhead · Khandala viewpoints · Tamhini Ghat top

RE Himalayan Tips

⛽

Fuel Stops

Fill up in Lonavala, Khopoli, and Pen. Kolad has one petrol station — verify it's open before arriving. The Himalayan's 15L tank gives ~300km range so anxiety is unnecessary.

🔧

Tyre Pressure

Run slightly lower pressure (28–30 PSI) on the ghat roads and Rajmachi off-road. Bump back up to 32–34 PSI for the Expressway return.

🌧️

April Weather

Pre-monsoon heat. Mornings are pleasant (22–26°C), afternoons can be hot (32–36°C). Ride early, break midday. Light rain showers possible by afternoon — pack a rain layer.

🪨

Road Conditions

Old highway and ghat sections have potholes and sand patches. NH48 and NH66 are excellent. Borghat descent: watch for trucks, fallen rocks, and damp patches in shadow.

📍

Navigation

Download Google Maps offline for the Lonavala region before leaving. Cell signal is patchy inside the ghats and near Rajmachi. Karla and Bhaja are well-signposted.

🎒

Packing

Saddle bags or a tank bag suit the Himalayan well. Pack: rain cover, basic tools, first aid kit, water (2L), sunscreen, and a fleece for cool ghat mornings.
